Amazon Sales for Africa (2010 - 2017)

Tools: Tableau

Introduction

Amazon is the world’s largest online retailer, offering a wide range of products, services, and digital content. It also provides cloud computing services through Amazon Web Services (AWS), the largest cloud infrastructure platform globally. Amazon relies heavily on data-driven decision-making to optimize operations and increase profitability. This project leverages Amazon sales data from 2010 to 2017 to develop an interactive Tableau dashboard that uncovers key insights and trends regarding Amazon’s sales performance in African countries.

Key Data Questions Addressed

The Tableau dashboard provides insights into the following key questions:

  • What is the most profitable sales channel in Africa?
  • Which African country generated the highest revenue for Amazon during the study period?
  • Which product category had the highest unit price among all items sold in Africa?
  • Which year recorded the highest number of units sold?

Summary of Findings

  • The offline sales channel proved to be the most profitable, accounting for 62% of all Amazon sales in Africa during the period under review.
  • Mozambique emerged as the top revenue-generating country, contributing $3,586,605, followed closely by Sierra Leone, which generated $2,868,580 in total revenue.
  • The Office Supplies category had the highest unit price, with an average cost of $3,256 per unit.
  • The year 2012 recorded the highest number of units sold, with a total of 37,797 orders placed across Africa.
